Participation of Women in the Workforce and Educational Attainments: Wage Gap Realities

India's GDP increased dramatically throughout the first decade of the twenty-first century. During this decade, the overall population of women over the age of 15 increased by 86.5 million. However, only 8.9% entered the labor force, and only 7.5% found active employment (Chandrasekhar & Ghosh, 2011). Despite significant GDP growth and increased job prospects, it did not drive many women to enter the labor field.

Conclusion

In India, there is a persistent gender wage disparity. Women tend to earn less than men in a variety of industries, and their labor-force participation remains disproportionately low. Despite economic development and expanded work options, this wage disparity persists.The pay gap is influenced by various factors, including sector-specific dynamics, education levels, and work status.Despite significant economic growth and increased job opportunities, the proportion of women in the labor force remains relatively low, indicating potential barriers to women's workforce.
